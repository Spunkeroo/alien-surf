Top Surf Destinations Around the World in 2026

Every surfer dreams of the perfect wave in a perfect setting. The world offers an extraordinary variety of surf destinations, from the thundering barrels of Hawaii's North Shore to the mellow beach breaks of Portugal's Algarve, from Indonesia's warm tropical perfection to the wild Atlantic swells of Ireland. Each destination offers a unique combination of wave quality, culture, cost, and experience.

This guide covers the top surf destinations around the world in 2026, organized by region. For each destination, we cover the best season, wave types, skill level required, estimated costs, and practical travel tips.

Hawaii, USA -- The Birthplace of Modern Surfing

Hawaii is where modern surfing was born, and it remains the spiritual home of the sport. The Hawaiian Islands offer every type of wave imaginable, from gentle beginner breaks to the most powerful waves on Earth. The North Shore of Oahu during winter is the epicenter of professional surfing, hosting competitions at Pipeline, Sunset Beach, and Haleiwa.

  • Best season: November to February for North Shore big waves; May to September for south-facing breaks and calmer conditions
  • Skill levels: All levels. Waikiki is perfect for beginners. The North Shore is for experienced to expert surfers only during winter.
  • Water temperature: 74-80F (23-27C) year-round. Board shorts or a thin rash guard are sufficient.
  • Top spots: Pipeline (expert barrel), Sunset Beach (powerful open face), Waikiki (beginner longboard), Rocky Point (intermediate), Laniakea (intermediate-advanced)
  • Budget: $150-300/night accommodation, $15-25/day board rental, $50-80/meal at restaurants. Hawaii is expensive.
  • Travel tip: Respect the locals. Hawaii's surf culture has deep roots, and locals are protective of their lineups. Be humble, wait your turn, and do not drop in on anyone.

Indonesia -- Bali, Mentawai & Beyond

Indonesia is a surfer's paradise with over 17,000 islands offering world-class waves in warm tropical water. Bali is the most accessible and popular, with breaks for every level from the gentle rollers of Kuta to the legendary barrels of Uluwatu and Padang Padang. The Mentawai Islands off Sumatra offer some of the most perfect waves on Earth, accessible by boat charter.

  • Best season: April to October (dry season) for Bali's west coast; year-round for the Mentawais
  • Skill levels: All levels in Bali (Kuta/Canggu for beginners, Uluwatu for advanced). Mentawais are intermediate to expert.
  • Water temperature: 78-84F (26-29C). Board shorts only.
  • Top spots: Uluwatu (Bali, expert left-hander), Canggu (Bali, beginner-intermediate), Padang Padang (Bali, expert barrel), Mentawai Islands (world-class barrels), G-Land (Java, expert left)
  • Budget: $15-80/night accommodation, $8-15/day board rental, $3-15/meal. Indonesia is one of the cheapest surf destinations.
  • Travel tip: Get a Bali SIM card at the airport for cheap data. Use Grab (ride-hailing app) for transport. Avoid surfing at Uluwatu until you can confidently handle overhead reef breaks.

Portugal -- Europe's Surf Capital

Portugal has established itself as Europe's premier surf destination, combining consistent Atlantic swells, affordable living costs, excellent food, and a growing surf culture. The coastline from the Algarve in the south to Nazare in the north offers 300+ days of surfable waves per year. Peniche, Ericeira, and the Algarve coast are the most popular surf areas.

  • Best season: September to November for the best combination of swell and weather. Winter (December-February) brings bigger swells but colder water. Summer is smaller but warm.
  • Skill levels: All levels. The Algarve has gentle beach breaks for beginners. Peniche and Ericeira offer intermediate to advanced reef and point breaks. Nazare is for tow-in big wave surfing only.
  • Water temperature: 57-68F (14-20C). A 3/2mm or 4/3mm wetsuit is needed most of the year.
  • Top spots: Supertubos (Peniche, expert barrel), Coxos (Ericeira, expert right), Arrifana (Algarve, beginner-intermediate), Nazare (big wave), Sagres (beginner)
  • Budget: $30-100/night accommodation, $10-20/day board rental, $10-25/meal. Very affordable for Western Europe.
  • Travel tip: Rent a car to explore the coastline. Portugal's best waves are spread across hundreds of kilometers, and a car gives you the flexibility to chase the best conditions.

Australia -- Endless Coastline, Endless Waves

Australia's 25,000+ kilometers of coastline deliver some of the most consistent and varied surfing on Earth. The Gold Coast's point breaks, Sydney's beach breaks, Margaret River's powerful swells, and the remote perfection of the Kimberley coast offer a lifetime of surf exploration. Australian surf culture runs deep -- surfing is a mainstream lifestyle, not a niche pursuit.

  • Best season: March to November for the Gold Coast; year-round for most of the coast; April to October for Western Australia
  • Skill levels: All levels. Beginner beaches are abundant near every major coastal city.
  • Water temperature: 64-78F (18-26C) depending on region and season. 2mm to 4/3mm wetsuit depending on location.
  • Top spots: Snapper Rocks (Gold Coast, expert right point), Bells Beach (Victoria, iconic right), Margaret River (WA, powerful breaks), Byron Bay (NSW, beginner-friendly), Noosa (QLD, mellow longboard waves)
  • Budget: $50-150/night accommodation, $25-40/day board rental, $15-30/meal. Comparable to the US.
  • Travel tip: Watch for rip currents and swim between the flags at patrolled beaches. Australian lifeguards are world-class, but ocean conditions can be deceptive for visitors unfamiliar with the surf.

Costa Rica -- Warm Water, Both Coasts

Costa Rica is the most popular Central American surf destination, offering warm water, consistent waves, lush tropical scenery, and a relaxed Pura Vida culture. The Pacific coast receives the most consistent swell, with spots ranging from beginner-friendly beach breaks to expert reef breaks. The Caribbean coast offers shorter surf seasons but uncrowded waves and a completely different cultural vibe.

  • Best season: May to November (Pacific coast, rainy season brings bigger swells); December to March (Caribbean coast)
  • Skill levels: All levels. Tamarindo and Jaco are established beginner destinations. Witch's Rock and Ollie's Point are advanced.
  • Water temperature: 78-84F (26-29C). Board shorts year-round.
  • Top spots: Tamarindo (beginner), Playa Hermosa (intermediate-advanced), Witch's Rock (advanced), Pavones (one of the world's longest lefts), Salsa Brava (Caribbean, expert)
  • Budget: $30-100/night accommodation, $10-20/day board rental, $5-15/meal. Moderate cost.

Sri Lanka -- The Emerging Gem

Sri Lanka has emerged as one of the best-value surf destinations in the world. The southern and eastern coasts offer warm water waves for all levels, with a fraction of the crowds found in Bali or Costa Rica. The island's compact size means you can surf the south coast in the morning and explore ancient temples inland in the afternoon. Food is exceptional and cheap.

  • Best season: November to April for the south coast (Weligama, Mirissa, Hiriketiya); May to October for the east coast (Arugam Bay)
  • Skill levels: Primarily beginner to intermediate. Weligama is one of the world's best beginner surf spots.
  • Water temperature: 79-84F (26-29C). No wetsuit needed.
  • Top spots: Weligama (beginner paradise), Arugam Bay (intermediate right point), Hiriketiya (beginner-intermediate bay), Mirissa (intermediate), Lazy Left (intermediate)
  • Budget: $10-50/night accommodation, $5-10/day board rental, $2-8/meal. One of the cheapest surf destinations.

Morocco -- Surf & Culture

Morocco's Atlantic coast receives powerful, consistent swells that create world-class right-hand point breaks along a dramatic desert coastline. The Taghazout area north of Agadir is the hub of Moroccan surf tourism, with a cluster of excellent waves within a short drive. The combination of cheap living, warm hospitality, incredible food, and reliable waves makes Morocco a top value destination.

  • Best season: October to March for the biggest and most consistent swells. September and April offer smaller but less crowded conditions.
  • Skill levels: Intermediate to advanced for the point breaks. Beginners can find beach breaks near Agadir and Essaouira.
  • Water temperature: 61-70F (16-21C). A 3/2mm wetsuit is recommended.
  • Top spots: Anchor Point (expert right), Killer Point (advanced right), Banana Beach (beginner), Taghazout beach (beginner-intermediate), Imsouane (longest right in Africa)
  • Budget: $15-60/night accommodation, $8-15/day board rental, $3-10/meal. Very affordable.

California, USA -- The American Dream

California's 1,100-mile coastline offers extraordinary variety: the big waves and cold water of Northern California, the consistent beach breaks of Central California, and the mellow warm-water breaks of Southern California. Surf culture permeates California life, with world-class breaks accessible from major cities.

  • Best season: August to November for Southern California (south swells plus north swells); October to April for Northern California (large northwest swells)
  • Skill levels: All levels. San Onofre, Doheny, and Linda Mar are beginner-friendly. Mavericks and the Wedge are expert only.
  • Water temperature: 50-70F (10-21C) depending on region. Full wetsuit required year-round in NorCal; spring suit or trunks in summer SoCal.
  • Top spots: Trestles (San Clemente, all levels), Rincon (Santa Barbara, expert right point), Mavericks (Half Moon Bay, big wave), Huntington Beach (intermediate), Steamer Lane (Santa Cruz, intermediate-advanced)
  • Budget: $80-250/night accommodation. California is expensive, especially coastal areas.

Maldives -- Luxury Perfection

The Maldives offer some of the most perfect, uncrowded waves on Earth, breaking over shallow coral reefs in crystal-clear warm water. Access is typically by boat charter or surf resort, which limits crowds but increases cost. For surfers who can afford it, the Maldives represent the pinnacle of the surf travel experience.

  • Best season: March to October (southwest monsoon brings consistent swells)
  • Skill levels: Intermediate to expert. Reef breaks over shallow coral require confidence and experience.
  • Water temperature: 82-86F (28-30C). Board shorts only.
  • Top spots: Pasta Point (North Male Atoll, resort-access right), Sultans (Central Atolls, right), Cokes (left), Chickens (right)
  • Budget: $150-500/night accommodation, boat charters $200-400/day. This is a premium destination.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best surf destination for beginners?

Sri Lanka (Weligama and Mirissa) and Portugal (the Algarve coast) are the best surf destinations for beginners. Both offer consistent small to medium waves, affordable surf schools, cheap accommodation, and a welcoming culture. Bali's Kuta Beach and Costa Rica's Tamarindo are also excellent beginner-friendly destinations.

When is the best time to surf in Bali?

The best time to surf in Bali is during the dry season from April to October. The west coast receives consistent swells from the Indian Ocean during this period. The east coast is better during the wet season (November to March). Bali offers surfable waves year-round.

How much does a surf trip cost?

A two-week surf trip to Bali or Sri Lanka can cost $1,500-2,500 including flights. Portugal costs $2,000-3,500. Hawaii is $3,000-5,000. The Maldives or Mentawai boat trips can run $3,000-6,000+ for 10 days.

Do I need to bring my own surfboard on a surf trip?

For most destinations, renting boards locally is easier and cheaper. Board bag airline fees run $50-200 each way. Popular destinations have abundant board rental shops with quality boards for $10-25 per day. Bring your own only for advanced surfing with specific board requirements or remote areas.

What is the most dangerous surf destination?

Hawaii's North Shore (Pipeline, Sunset Beach) is the most dangerous well-known surf destination due to powerful waves over shallow reef. Teahupo'o in Tahiti and Nazare in Portugal produce extremely heavy waves. These are for expert surfers only. Every destination has safe beginner spots -- the danger comes from surfing beyond your ability.

Conclusion

The world offers an incredible diversity of surf destinations, and 2026 is the best time in history to plan a surf trip. Budget destinations like Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and Morocco make surf travel accessible to everyone. Established destinations like Hawaii, Australia, and Portugal continue to deliver world-class waves with excellent infrastructure. And premium destinations like the Maldives offer the trip of a lifetime for those who can invest in it.

The best surf destination is the one that matches your skill level, budget, and travel style. Start with a destination that has reliable beginner waves and affordable living, and work your way up to more challenging destinations as your surfing progresses.
